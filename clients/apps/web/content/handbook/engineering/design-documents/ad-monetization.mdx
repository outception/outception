## Abstract

Outception is a live news wall shown mostly through the **iOS App Store and Google Play** apps (web traffic is minor). We monetize by **showing Google ads to readers** rather than **selling anything**. This removes the merchant-of-record/VAT problem that blocked pay-to-promote: as an ad publisher we receive payouts from Google (ordinary income), and we are never the seller of a product.

- **Mobile (primary):** Google **AdMob** via `react-native-google-mobile-ads` in the Expo app.
- **Web (secondary):** Google **AdSense** in the Next.js app.
- **Paid promotion is retired.** Featured slots become AdMob **native ads** (mobile) / AdSense units (web) or **free, vote-ranked community boosts**.

## Why this is the right model

- **No MoR / no VAT-on-sales.** We don't sell — Google bills advertisers and pays us. (Contrast: selling promotion made us a merchant → the Polar rejection.)
- **Fits the traffic shape.** Ad revenue is a volume game; app-store installs are exactly the volume an ad model wants.
- **Even a future "remove ads" upgrade stays MoR-free** — it would go through App Store / Play **IAP**, where Apple/Google are the merchant of record. (Out of scope now; noted for later.)

## What we need (all free)

- **Google AdMob** account (mobile apps + ad units).
- **Google AdSense** account (web) — note the stricter review (see Risks).
- A **Google-certified Consent Management Platform (CMP)** for EEA/UK: **Google UMP** on mobile, a certified CMP banner on web. Required by Google to serve ads in the EEA/UK.
- **iOS App Tracking Transparency (ATT)** prompt — Apple rejects ad apps without it.
- A **privacy policy** page (stores + Google require it; must disclose ad data use).
- An **EAS build pipeline** for the mobile app (native ad SDK can't run in Expo Go).

No Stripe, no Polar, no webhook billing for this model.

## Policy compliance (must-follow or you get struck/rejected)

- **Aggregator apps are allowed on AdMob** provided we follow AdSense program policies. Keep the product **headlines + link-out to the source** — do **not** start reproducing full third-party articles under ads ("framed content already monetized by Google" is disallowed).
- **Consent:** serve EEA/UK users only after CMP consent (UMP mobile / certified CMP web). Non-consented users get non-personalized ads only.
- **ATT (iOS):** show the prompt; personalized ads only if granted.
- **Ad UX:** no interstitials on app open every session, no accidental-click layouts, no ads over content — Apple/Google both penalize this.
- **Privacy labels:** update App Store "Privacy" + Play "Data safety" to declare advertising/identifier usage.

## Mobile plan — `apps/app` (Expo / React Native), primary

1. **AdMob setup:** create AdMob account → register the iOS and Android apps → create ad units (app-open, banner, native, interstitial). App IDs look like `ca-app-pub-XXXX~YYYY`.
2. **SDK integration:** add `react-native-google-mobile-ads` (+ `@react-native-async-storage/async-storage`); configure the plugin + `android_app_id` / `ios_app_id` in `app.config.js`. This requires an **EAS development build** (the app isn't device-tested yet, so we stand up EAS regardless).
3. **Consent & privacy (gating first launch):**
   - **UMP** consent form (`AdsConsent` from the SDK) before requesting ads.
   - **ATT** via `expo-tracking-transparency` on iOS.
   - Set `requestNonPersonalizedAdsOnly` when consent/ATT is not granted.
4. **Placements (revenue-optimized for a scroll app):**
   - **Native ads interspersed in the deck** every ~6–8 cards (best fit; reads like a promoted card).
   - **Anchored banner** at the bottom of the feed.
   - **Interstitial** used sparingly (e.g. at natural breaks), never on cold start.
5. **Store submission:** privacy labels, ATT usage string in `Info.plist`, Play Data-safety form.

## Web plan — `apps/web` (Next.js), secondary

1. **AdSense account + site review** (approval risk — see Risks). Add the AdSense script (via `next/script`, `afterInteractive`) in the root layout, gated behind consent.
2. **Certified CMP** banner for EEA/UK (Google's own consent or a certified vendor); block ad requests until a choice is made. The app already derives `GEO_COUNTRY` — reuse it to decide whether the CMP is required.
3. **Placements:** an in-feed unit every N cards on the news wall (mirroring mobile), plus a sidebar/leaderboard on wide layouts. Keep the feed's Orbit layout; ad slots are `<ins class="adsbygoogle">` wrapped in a Box container.
4. Because web traffic is low, this is **phase 2** — ship mobile ads first.

## Consent, privacy & data

- **Mobile:** UMP + ATT as above; store privacy labels updated.
- **Web:** certified CMP; `adsbygoogle` requests gated on consent.
- **Privacy policy** page (web + linked from app stores) disclosing Google ad partners, identifiers, and EEA choices. Add/refresh it.
- Personalized vs non-personalized ads switch on consent state everywhere.

## Config / env

- **Mobile** (`app.config.js`, public): `ADMOB_IOS_APP_ID`, `ADMOB_ANDROID_APP_ID`, and per-format ad-unit IDs (public identifiers, safe in the client; use Google **test IDs** in dev).
- **Web** (`NEXT_PUBLIC_ADSENSE_CLIENT`, ad-slot IDs): public.
- No secrets, no deploy-secret injection needed for ads (unlike the payment creds).

## Phased rollout

- **Phase 1 — Mobile ads (primary):** EAS build + `react-native-google-mobile-ads` + UMP + ATT + native/banner placements; ship to TestFlight/internal track, then stores.
- **Phase 2 — Web ads:** AdSense approval + CMP + in-feed units.
- **Phase 3 — Retire paid promotion:** swap the featured slot to ads/free boosts; disable checkout; later remove billing/Polar code + config.
- **Phase 4 (optional, later):** "Remove ads" upgrade via App Store/Play **IAP** (Apple/Google = MoR).

## Risks & open questions

- **AdSense web approval** is stricter in 2026 (helpful-content signals) and can reject thin/aggregated sites. Mitigate: link-out model, add original framing/curation, or **skip web ads** if not approved — mobile carries the model.
- **AdMob policy strike risk** if the product drifts toward reproducing full articles. Keep headlines + link-out.
- **Revenue depends on install volume** and geo mix; aggregator eCPMs are modest. Validate with a small launch before over-investing.
- **iOS review:** ATT string + real native functionality required (the app clears this; a "just a website" build would be rejected).
- **EEA consent correctness** is a compliance must — get the CMP wiring right before first ad request.
- **Mobile app maturity:** not yet device-tested / no EAS build on record — Phase 1 includes standing that up.

## Summary

Show Google ads instead of selling promotion. Mobile AdMob is the engine (that's where the users are), web AdSense is a bonus, and paid promotion is retired into native-ad or free-boost slots. Net effect: a monetization path with **no merchant-of-record, no VAT, no payment provider** — just publisher payouts from Google — matched to an app-store-first distribution.
